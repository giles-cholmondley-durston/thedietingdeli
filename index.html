What is Keto?

A ketogenic diet is high in good fats, moderate in protein, and low in sugars and carbohydrates. This diet helps the body achieve a metabolic state called ketosis, using ketones produced in the liver for energy rather than glucose from carbs. The body works harder to break down ketones and burns more fat.

Always seek the advice of your physician or a qualified health care provider with any questions regarding your health, diet, or fitness. Green Chef does not provide medical, dietary, or fitness advice.

Is Green Chef Keto Right For Me?

Green Chef Keto can be for anybody looking to limit carbs. If you follow a ketogenic lifestyle and want to add delicious variety to the mix, this is the perfect fit. Or if you’re simply keto-curious, Green Chef Keto can offer you a taste of eating ketogenic.

You’re sure to love Green Chef Keto if you’re already a fan of our paleo dinners. Like paleo, our delicious keto-friendly meals have no wheat, gluten, or grains.

We make it easy to switch between meal plans week to week and encourage you to experience Green Chef Keto yourself. After all, variety is the…well, you know!

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What ingredients are part of Green Chef’s Keto Plan?

    Green Chef Keto has NO:

    Grains: wheat, corn, rice, cereal, etc.

    Excess sugar: refined sugar, honey, agave, maple syrup, etc.

    Fruit: apples, bananas, oranges, etc.

    Tubers: potatoes, yams, etc.

    Farmed seafood: shrimp, salmon, trout, etc.

    Gluten

    Peanuts

    Soy

    Green Chef Keto has:

    Meat: beef, pork, poultry, lamb, game

    Seafood: wild-caught fish

    Leafy greens: spinach, kale, etc.

    Above-ground vegetables: broccoli, cauliflower, etc.

    Limited root vegetables: carrots

    Nuts and seeds: macadamias, walnuts, sunflower seeds, etc.

    Avocados and berries: raspberries, blackberries, and other low-glycemic berries

    Sweeteners: stevia, erythritol, monk fruit, etc.

    Eggs

    Tree nuts

  • How can I hit my nutrient macros with Green Chef Keto?

    Green Chef Keto meals generally fall into a range of 400-600 calories and typically contain less than 30g net carbs per serving. Full nutritional info is provided online for each recipe. Our meals are designed to be keto-friendly, not keto-stringent. To hit a desired fat profile, consider cooking with saturated fats like coconut oil, butter, or ghee. We provide plenty of vegetables, so you can limit carbs further and still enjoy a filling meal by saving some veggies for later.

  • What are net carbs?

    We use the term “net carbs” to estimate total carbohydrate content minus the fiber content of the meal.
